How Our Custom Floral Design Process Works

Great floral design begins with listening. Before a single stem is selected, we learn what you need, where the flowers will go, and what atmosphere you want to create. That discovery process allows us to build arrangements with meaning and visual balance.

1. Consultation and concept

We begin by understanding the event type, color preferences, floral likes and dislikes, and any practical details such as delivery timing, venue size, or placement. A custom wedding piece will be approached differently than a corporate reception arrangement or a home décor installation.

2. Flower selection

Next, we choose blooms based on seasonality, texture, color story, and durability. Roses, peonies, orchids, hydrangeas, tulips, lilies, ranunculus, and greenery all play different roles in the final composition. A well-designed bouquet is not just a collection of flowers; it is a composition with rhythm, contrast, and movement.

3. Arrangement and refinement

Once the flowers are selected, our designers craft the arrangement by balancing focal blooms, supporting flowers, and foliage. We refine the shape so it looks beautiful from the key viewing angles and performs well in the intended setting.

How to Choose the Right Custom Floral Arrangement

If you are ordering custom florals for the first time, keep these points in mind:

  1. Start with the purpose. Decide whether the arrangement is for gifting, décor, sympathy, celebration, or branding.
  2. Consider the setting. A shaded indoor room, a bright storefront, and an outdoor patio all require different design choices.
  3. Think about color. Choose tones that reflect the occasion and complement the space.
  4. Match the scale. Large venues may need more dramatic designs, while intimate spaces often look best with compact, refined arrangements.
  5. Trust the season. Seasonal flowers often provide the best value, freshness, and visual impact.
