How to Choose the Right Mother’s Day Bouquet

If you’re not sure what to send, start by thinking about the recipient’s style and how she’ll use the flowers. Is she someone who loves a dramatic statement piece, or does she prefer something simple and graceful? Will the flowers be placed on a dining table, a desk, a bedside table, or near a sunny window?

Consider Her Favorite Colors

Many customers begin with color because it makes the gift feel personal. If she loves warm tones, go with peach, coral, and golden yellow. If she prefers calm and classic design, choose ivory, blush, and soft green. For a more modern look, deeper jewel tones can create a striking effect.

Think About Fragrance

Some mothers love fragrant blooms like lilies, stock, or garden roses. Others prefer low-fragrance designs for comfort in the home. We can help guide you toward the right balance so the bouquet feels enjoyable rather than overpowering.

Match the Arrangement to the Setting

A large, abundant centerpiece may be perfect for a Mother’s Day lunch in a spacious Chatsworth dining room, while a compact vase arrangement may suit a condo, apartment, or office setting better. The right scale makes the gift more practical and more beautiful.
