Funeral Flower Etiquette and Local Considerations

When sending funeral flowers, etiquette matters. In many cases, the sender wants to show support without overwhelming the family or violating the tone of the service. Glendale’s diverse community includes families with different customs, so it is wise to consider both the type of arrangement and the relationship to the deceased.

Helpful etiquette tips

  • Send larger pieces, such as casket sprays, only if you are an immediate family member or have been asked to do so.
  • Use sympathy bouquets for homes, offices, or when a smaller gesture is more appropriate.
  • Choose white or softly colored flowers for a traditional and respectful presentation.
  • Include a clear card message with the recipient’s name and the service details.
  • Order early when possible, especially for weekday services or same-day needs in busy Glendale traffic.

For families near the foothills, where afternoon sun and warmer conditions can be more intense, flower durability becomes even more important. For indoor services in cooler, shaded venues, a wider range of blooms may be appropriate. These small local factors can make a big difference in how a tribute looks throughout the service.
