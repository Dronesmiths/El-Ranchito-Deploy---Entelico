Guide to Sympathy Flowers and Etiquette

During times of loss, words often fall short. Flowers have been used for centuries as a universal language of sympathy, offering comfort, respect, and a visual representation of love for the departed. At Flowers El Ranchito, we handle funeral arrangements with the utmost care and sensitivity.

Understanding Funeral Flower Etiquette

Understanding funeral flower etiquette is important. Arrangements sent directly to a funeral home or church (like standing wreaths or casket sprays) are meant to be large and formal. Arrangements sent to the family's home should be smaller, elegant, and designed to bring warmth to the household.

Custom Tributes and Personalized Arrangements

We specialize in creating custom floral tributes that celebrate the life of the deceased. Whether it's incorporating their favorite colors, integrating elements of their hobbies, or crafting a specific shape like a heart or a cross, we help you honor their memory.

Quick Answers (FAQ)

What is the appropriate flower to send for a funeral?

Lilies, carnations, chrysanthemums, and roses are the most traditional and appropriate flowers for funerals, symbolizing peace, purity, and love.

Should I send flowers to the funeral home or the family's house?

Send large standing sprays or wreaths to the funeral home for the service. Send smaller, vase-arranged sympathy bouquets to the family's home.

Is it ever too late to send sympathy flowers?

No, it is never too late. If you missed the funeral, sending a thoughtful arrangement to the family's home a week or two later is a beautiful gesture showing continued support.
