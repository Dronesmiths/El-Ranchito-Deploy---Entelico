Casket Sprays and Standing Wreaths

When attending or planning a funeral service, the large floral displays placed around the casket are the most prominent expressions of love. Understanding the specific roles of these arrangements ensures proper etiquette.

The Role of the Casket Spray

The Casket Spray is the most significant floral tribute at a funeral. This large, sweeping arrangement rests directly on top of the casket. According to tradition, the casket spray is strictly ordered and paid for by the immediate family (spouse, children, or parents).

Standing Wreaths and Crosses

For extended family, friends, and colleagues, Standing Wreaths, Crosses, and Hearts are the appropriate choice. These large arrangements are mounted on easels and placed around the casket. They provide a beautiful backdrop for the service and can be customized with banners bearing personalized messages.

Quick Answers (FAQ)

Who is supposed to buy the casket spray?

The casket spray is traditionally purchased by the closest immediate family members, such as the spouse, children, or parents of the deceased.

What is the difference between a half-couch and full-couch casket spray?

A half-couch spray covers the lower half of an open casket, while a full-couch spray is larger and designed to be placed on a completely closed casket.

Can coworkers chip in to buy a standing wreath?

Yes, it is very common for coworkers or social clubs to pool their money to purchase a large standing wreath or easel spray on behalf of the group.
