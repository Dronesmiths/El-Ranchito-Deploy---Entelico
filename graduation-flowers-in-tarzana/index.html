Graduation Flower Etiquette: What to Send and When

If you’re unsure what’s appropriate, you’re not alone. Graduation flowers can be personal, but they also follow a few simple etiquette principles. The goal is to celebrate without making things complicated for the graduate.

When to send flowers

Many people send flowers on graduation day itself so the bouquet can be part of the moment. Others prefer delivery the day before, especially if the graduate is traveling between events or needs time to prepare for photos. For home celebrations, same-day gifting can work beautifully. For formal ceremonies, earlier delivery helps avoid last-minute stress.

What size is best?

A medium bouquet is often the sweet spot for graduations. It feels substantial enough for the occasion without becoming awkward to carry. Larger arrangements work well for home displays or family gifting, while smaller arrangements can be perfect for a subtle and stylish presentation. If you’re celebrating a graduate from a Tarzana household that expects multiple guests, you may also want a second arrangement for the table or entryway.

Should you add extras?

In many cases, yes. A handwritten card, a ribbon in school colors, or a vase upgrade can make the gift feel more complete. For graduates who will be moving between events, a sturdy presentation box or wrapped bouquet may be the most practical option.
