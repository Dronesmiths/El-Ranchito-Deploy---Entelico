Accessibility Standards We Aim to Follow

Our website is designed with the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines, commonly known as WCAG 2.1, in mind. These guidelines are widely recognized as a benchmark for digital accessibility and focus on making web content perceivable, operable, understandable, and robust. In practical terms, that means we work to ensure the site functions well for people who cannot use a mouse, who rely on text alternatives, or who need a clear and predictable interface.

What WCAG 2.1 Means for Visitors

  • Text should be readable and legible against its background.
  • Images should include meaningful alternative text where appropriate.
  • Navigation should be usable with a keyboard.
  • Interactive elements should have clear labels and focus states.
  • Content should be organized logically with headings and structure.
  • Forms and buttons should be understandable and easy to complete.

We understand that accessibility needs can vary widely. For someone in Sun Valley dealing with glare and heat, a simpler mobile interface may matter most. For another customer in Sherman Oaks using a screen reader, semantic structure and descriptive labels may be essential. That is why we review the site from multiple user perspectives rather than relying on a single design standard.

How We Support Accessible Browsing

To make the site more usable, we focus on practical improvements that help people find information quickly and complete tasks without unnecessary friction. This includes page structure, readable content, and sensible interaction patterns that support accessibility across modern browsers and devices.

Key Accessibility Practices

  1. Clear page hierarchy: We use headings to organize content so visitors can scan pages efficiently.
  2. Keyboard-friendly navigation: We aim to ensure users can move through the site without requiring a mouse.
  3. Text alternatives: Important images are reviewed for descriptive alternative text when appropriate.
  4. Readable language: We write content in plain language wherever possible to reduce confusion.
  5. Responsive layout: We strive for a mobile-friendly experience for people browsing on phones and tablets.
  6. Consistent design: We work to keep interface elements predictable so visitors know what to expect.
Accessibility is more than compliance. It is a commitment to hospitality, clarity, and respect for every visitor who wants to connect with Flowers El Ranchito.

In a region where users may browse while traveling between Pasadena and the Valley, or while managing busy schedules around Ventura Boulevard, accessibility must work under real-life conditions. People may be using one hand on a phone, reduced brightness settings, or assistive software. We recognize those realities and try to build a site that remains functional and welcoming.

Known Limitations and Ongoing Improvements

Despite our best efforts, some areas of the website may not yet be fully accessible to every user.
