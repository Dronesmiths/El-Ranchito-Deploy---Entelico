How to Choose the Right Bouquet for Your Wedding Vision

Choosing the right bouquet starts with asking the right questions. Do you want something soft and romantic, or crisp and architectural? Will the bouquet need to match a lace gown, satin dress, or modern minimalist silhouette? Is the wedding happening outdoors in the heat or indoors with controlled climate? These details influence the final design.

Consider Your Dress

A bouquet should complement, not compete with, the wedding dress. A detailed gown often pairs best with a bouquet that is elegant but not overly busy. A simple dress may allow for a more dramatic arrangement with strong color and texture.

Consider the Venue

Sylmar offers a range of wedding settings, and each one creates a different floral opportunity. A hillside venue may call for organic movement and soft neutrals. A formal reception hall may call for tighter structure and high-impact blooms. Even the drive to the venue matters, especially for weddings navigating traffic along the 210 or through neighborhood streets with limited parking.

Consider the Time of Day

Morning ceremonies, late afternoon vows, and evening receptions all affect how flowers behave and how they are seen. Bright midday light can flatten pale tones, while evening events can enrich deep colors. We account for this during design so your bouquet looks right in real life and in photos.
