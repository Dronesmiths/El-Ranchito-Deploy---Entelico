How Our Custom Design Process Works

Great floral design starts with listening. We want to understand the occasion, your style preferences, your budget, and where the arrangement will be displayed. Then we select flowers and structure the design so it feels cohesive and intentional.

1. Style and Purpose

We begin by identifying the goal. Is the arrangement meant to impress, comfort, celebrate, or decorate? A floral design for a restaurant on a busy San Fernando route needs a different shape and durability than flowers for a private home near the quieter residential pockets of the city.

2. Color and Texture

Color sets the tone. Soft pastels feel romantic and delicate, while saturated reds, oranges, and purples create drama and warmth. Texture adds dimension through roses, lilies, orchids, greenery, and accent blooms. We combine these elements to create visual interest without making the arrangement feel cluttered.

3. Scale and Placement

Size matters. A tall arrangement can anchor an entry table, while a low centerpiece works better for conversation at dinner. We consider ceiling height, table size, counter space, and traffic patterns so the design fits naturally into the setting.
