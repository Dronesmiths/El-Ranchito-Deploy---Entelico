How to Choose the Right Graduation Flowers

Choosing graduation flowers is easier when you start with the graduate’s style, the event setting, and the message you want to send. Some people want a dramatic bouquet that stands out in photos. Others prefer something refined and understated. A few key considerations can help narrow the options.

  1. Match the graduate’s personality. Bold graduates may love bright mixed blooms, while minimalist styles may suit whites, greens, and simple wrapping.
  2. Consider the venue. Outdoor Chatsworth celebrations in warm weather may call for hardy blooms and compact designs.
  3. Use school colors thoughtfully. A subtle ribbon or accent flower can create a custom look without overwhelming the arrangement.
  4. Think about transport. If the bouquet will travel across the Valley, choose a design that stays secure and easy to carry.
  5. Plan for photos. Clean lines, fresh blooms, and a polished finish help the flowers shine in pictures.
