Types of Funeral Arrangements We Create

Funeral flower arrangements come in many forms, and each one serves a different purpose. Choosing the right style depends on the relationship to the deceased, the type of service, and the message you want to convey. Below is a helpful overview of common options.

Arrangement Type Best For Typical Style
Casket Spray Immediate family Large, graceful floral piece placed on the casket
Standing Spray Funeral home or memorial service Vertical display on an easel for visibility
Wreath Sympathy tribute Round symbol of eternal remembrance
Sympathy Bouquet Home delivery or service attendance Smaller, thoughtful arrangement with a personal message
Cross or Heart Design Faith-based or deeply personal tribute Symbolic floral form with meaningful presence

Casket sprays

Casket sprays are among the most visible tributes at a funeral. They are typically selected by close family members and are designed to rest gracefully on the casket. These arrangements often include roses, lilies, orchids, carnations, hydrangeas, or seasonal accent flowers, depending on the desired look.

Standing sprays and easel pieces

Standing sprays are a classic choice for services at funeral homes and churches. Their vertical presentation makes them highly visible, which is especially meaningful in larger spaces or when many guests attend.

Sympathy arrangements for the home

Not every floral tribute needs to be displayed at the service itself. Many families and friends send sympathy flowers directly to the home as a quiet expression of support. These pieces are often softer in scale and more intimate in design.

How to Choose the Right Funeral Flowers

Selecting funeral flowers can feel intimidating if you have never ordered them before. The most important considerations are the relationship to the deceased, the tone of the service, and any preferences expressed by the family or faith tradition.

Consider the symbolism

Different flowers carry different meanings. Lilies are often associated with peace and restored innocence. Roses can convey love, respect, and remembrance. Carnations are known for their longevity and versatility. Orchids can symbolize enduring beauty and admiration. Choosing flowers with symbolic meaning can make the tribute feel even more personal.

Match the service style

A traditional chapel service may call for classic white arrangements, while a more contemporary memorial may welcome richer color palettes or modern floral styling. If the family has requested a specific theme, we can help align the arrangement with that vision.

Think about durability

Chatsworth’s dry valley heat can be tough on florals, especially during warm afternoons or when arrangements are displayed in direct sunlight near windows, entryways, or outdoor reception areas. We recommend blooms and design techniques that help maintain freshness and structure throughout the service.
