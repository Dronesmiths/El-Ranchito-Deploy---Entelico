How to Choose the Right Mother’s Day Flowers

Picking flowers is easier when you think beyond color alone. A great arrangement should reflect the recipient’s style, the intended setting, and how long you want the flowers to last.

“The best Mother’s Day bouquet is one that feels like it was chosen specifically for her — not just for the holiday.”

Consider her style

Does she prefer classic, romantic, modern, garden-inspired, or bright and energetic designs? A mother who loves understated décor may appreciate a clean arrangement of white lilies and soft pink roses. Someone with a bold personality may prefer rich jewel tones or a vibrant mix of spring flowers.

Think about the display location

If flowers are going to a kitchen counter in a Mission Hills ranch-style home, a medium-height arrangement may work best. If they’re being delivered to an office near the 405 corridor, a more compact design can be easier to place on a desk. For larger dining rooms or family gathering spaces, a fuller centerpiece can make a stronger impression.

Choose blooms with staying power

Because Mother’s Day delivery can involve warm weather and busy schedules, long-lasting blooms are always a smart choice. Roses, orchids, alstroemeria, carnations, chrysanthemums, and certain lilies can offer excellent value and durability when arranged professionally.
