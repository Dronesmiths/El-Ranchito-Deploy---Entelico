Care Tips for Tropical Flower Arrangements

Tropical flowers are known for their resilience, but a little care goes a long way. If you are receiving an arrangement, these simple steps can help extend its beauty.

  1. Keep the arrangement away from direct sunlight and hot windows.
  2. Refill water as needed and follow any care instructions included with the design.
  3. Place it in a stable, cool indoor area away from vents or appliances.
  4. Remove any fading blooms promptly to maintain the overall appearance.
  5. If the arrangement includes a vase, make sure it remains clean and properly filled.

These basics are especially helpful in North Hills, where indoor temperatures can rise quickly during Valley heat waves. Tropical flowers are hardy, but they still perform best when kept in a comfortable indoor environment.

Many tropical flowers last longer than standard cut flowers when properly cared for, especially orchids, anthuriums, and birds of paradise. Their lifespan depends on room temperature, water quality, and how the arrangement is maintained after delivery.
