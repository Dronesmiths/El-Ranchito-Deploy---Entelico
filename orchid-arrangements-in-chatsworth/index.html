How to Care for Orchid Arrangements

Proper care helps orchids remain beautiful longer, especially in Chatsworth’s dry valley climate. While orchids are generally low maintenance, a few thoughtful habits can make a big difference in how they perform indoors.

Keep Them Out of Harsh Direct Sun

Orchids prefer bright, indirect light. In Chatsworth homes with large windows facing strong afternoon sun, it is best to place arrangements a few feet back from the glass or in a filtered-light area. This helps preserve bloom quality and prevents stress from heat exposure.

Avoid Dry Heat Vents and Drafts

Air conditioning vents, heaters, and open doors can dry out flowers prematurely. In valley homes and offices where indoor cooling is common, placing orchids away from direct airflow helps maintain their freshness and structure.

Water with Care

Orchids do best with moderate watering and proper drainage. Overwatering can be more harmful than underwatering. If the arrangement includes a decorative container, make sure excess moisture does not collect at the base. A florist-designed orchid arrangement should always be supported by a structure that promotes healthy longevity.
