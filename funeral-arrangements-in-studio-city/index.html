Types of Funeral Arrangements We Provide

Funeral flowers come in many forms, each serving a different purpose in a memorial setting. The right selection depends on the relationship to the deceased, the style of the service, and the venue.

Standing Sprays and Easel Tributes

Standing sprays are one of the most traditional and visible forms of funeral flowers. They are designed to be displayed on an easel and can feature roses, lilies, carnations, orchids, or seasonal blooms. These arrangements are often chosen by immediate family members, close friends, or colleagues who want to make a meaningful statement of remembrance.

Casket Sprays

A casket spray is typically selected by the family and placed directly on the casket during the service. These designs may be full, half, or custom-sized depending on the type of casket and the family’s preferences. We create casket sprays that feel personal, elegant, and appropriate for both traditional and contemporary memorials.

Sympathy Bouquets and Vase Arrangements

For homes, visitation areas, or post-service gatherings, sympathy bouquets offer a softer expression of support. These arrangements can be sent to the family’s residence in Studio City, delivered to a place of worship, or placed at a memorial reception. They are often a thoughtful choice when you want to express condolences without overwhelming the setting.

Custom Memorial Tributes

Some families want a tribute that reflects a loved one’s hobbies, faith, profession, or personality. Custom pieces can incorporate specific flowers, colors, or symbolic shapes. We have created arrangements inspired by musicians, educators, veterans, grandparents, and beloved community members. In a neighborhood as creative as Studio City, personal details matter.

How to Choose the Right Funeral Flowers

If you are unsure what to order, start with the relationship to the deceased and the location of the service. Some arrangements are better suited for display at the funeral home, while others are intended for the family’s home or a memorial reception.

Consider the Service Type

  • Funeral home service: Standing sprays, casket sprays, and wreaths are often appropriate.
  • Church or chapel service: Larger pieces and altar arrangements may be ideal.
  • Private home condolence delivery: Vase arrangements and sympathy bouquets work well.
  • Memorial reception: Low arrangements that do not block conversation are often preferred.

Think About Color and Meaning

White flowers are a timeless choice for purity, peace, and remembrance. Red roses may symbolize love and devotion. Soft pinks can express tenderness, while blue and lavender tones create a calm, reflective atmosphere. If the family has requested a specific color scheme, we can match it carefully to the service environment.

Honor Cultural and Religious Traditions

Different traditions call for different floral approaches. Some services favor minimal arrangements, while others welcome abundant displays. We respect those differences and can help you select flowers that align with customs and expectations.

In moments of loss, the right flowers do not just decorate a room; they help tell a story of love, respect, and remembrance.
