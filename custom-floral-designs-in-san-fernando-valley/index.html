A custom arrangement can be modern and architectural, lush and romantic, minimal and refined, or bold and celebratory.

Our Custom Floral Design Process

Every project begins with a conversation. Some clients know exactly what they want. Others bring a few inspiration photos, a color palette, or even just a mood. We translate that starting point into a design that works beautifully in real life. Whether your floral needs are for a home in Tarzana, a business near the NoHo Arts District, or an event close to the 170, 405, 101, or 118 freeways, our process is designed to be efficient, creative, and dependable.

1. Discovery and Style Direction

We begin by learning the details: the occasion, preferred flowers, budget, delivery timing, and display location. We also consider the local environment—indoor air conditioning, outdoor heat, wind exposure, and traffic-sensitive delivery windows.

2. Flower Selection and Color Planning

Next, we build the palette. This may include roses, orchids, hydrangeas, lilies, ranunculus, peonies, tulips, premium greens, seasonal branches, or sculptural accent blooms. The final mix depends on the look you want and the longevity you need.

3. Hand Construction and Final Styling

Our floral artisans assemble each design with attention to proportion, movement, and balance. The result is not just a bouquet—it is a styled floral composition that photographs beautifully and feels intentional from every angle.

Choosing the Right Flowers for Valley Conditions

Not every bloom performs equally in Southern California heat. That is why flower selection matters so much. Some flowers are naturally better suited for warm climates and longer display times, while others are best used as accent blooms or for indoor-only arrangements.

Flowers That Often Perform Well

  • Roses for classic beauty and broad versatility
  • Orchids for elegance and strong visual presence
  • Carnations for durability and value
  • Alstroemeria for color and lasting performance
  • Chrysanthemums for fullness and resilience
  • Seasonal greens for texture and structure
