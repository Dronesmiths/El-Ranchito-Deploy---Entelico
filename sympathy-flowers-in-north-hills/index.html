Types of Sympathy Flowers We Offer

Sympathy flowers can express comfort in many ways, from soft pastel arrangements to elegant white tributes and formal memorial pieces. We offer a wide range of designs to support different traditions, preferences, and budgets.

Standing Sprays

Standing sprays are one of the most recognizable sympathy tributes. They are often displayed on an easel at memorial services and can be customized in color, flower type, and size. These arrangements are ideal when you want to make a strong, graceful statement of remembrance.

Casket Sprays

Casket sprays are typically selected by immediate family members and are designed to rest atop the casket during the service. These are often created with roses, lilies, orchids, carnations, and other elegant flowers that convey love and reverence.

Wreaths and Hearts

Floral wreaths and heart-shaped tributes symbolize eternal love and remembrance. They are especially meaningful when honoring a lifelong partner, parent, or close family member.

Sympathy Bouquets and Basket Arrangements

When to Send Sympathy Flowers

There is no single correct time to send sympathy flowers. Some people send them immediately after learning of a loss, while others wait until the service date or send a follow-up arrangement afterward. Each approach can be meaningful.

Immediately After a Loss

Sending flowers soon after hearing the news can provide immediate comfort. A simple basket arrangement or bouquet delivered to the home can let the family know they are not alone.

Before or During the Service

Many floral tributes are sent to arrive before the memorial or funeral. These pieces may be placed near the guest book, altar, or casket and become part of the service environment.

After the Service

Flowers sent later can be just as meaningful. They offer support during the quieter days after the memorial, when the family may still be receiving guests or adjusting to daily life without their loved one.
