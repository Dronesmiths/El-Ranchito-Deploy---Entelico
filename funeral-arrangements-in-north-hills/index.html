Types of Funeral Arrangements Available

Standing sprays

Standing sprays are among the most recognizable funeral flower designs. They are displayed on an easel and are often placed near the casket, podium, or entrance of the service. These arrangements can be formal and traditional with white lilies and roses, or more personal with color accents that reflect a loved one’s favorite flowers.

Casket flowers

Casket sprays are typically chosen by immediate family. These arrangements rest directly on the casket and can be full, half, or custom in shape. In North Hills services, families often choose classic white compositions, but soft pastels and richer tones are also appropriate depending on the person being honored.

Sympathy bouquets and vase arrangements

These are ideal for sending to a family’s home, the funeral home viewing room, or a memorial reception. Wreaths, hearts, and custom tributes

For a more symbolic expression, wreaths represent eternal life, while heart-shaped arrangements communicate deep personal love. Custom tributes can reflect hobbies, faith traditions, or personal identity. These pieces are especially meaningful when families want something that feels unique rather than standard.

Choosing the Right Flowers for a Funeral Tribute

Not every bloom carries the same meaning, and that matters when selecting funeral arrangements. The flowers chosen should align with the tone of the service and the message the family wants to share.

White lilies

White lilies are a timeless choice for funeral services because they symbolize peace, restored innocence, and reverence. They are frequently used in North Hills memorial arrangements because they create a serene, elegant appearance that suits both religious and secular services.

Roses

Roses remain one of the most versatile funeral flowers. White roses suggest purity and remembrance, red roses express love and respect, and pink roses can reflect gratitude and admiration. For families in North Hills who want a classic but personalized look, roses are an excellent foundation.

Chrysanthemums, carnations, and orchids

These flowers are often chosen for their longevity and symbolic value. Carnations hold up well in warm weather, chrysanthemums provide fullness and texture, and orchids can add a refined, modern note. In the Valley climate, these blooms are practical as well as beautiful.
