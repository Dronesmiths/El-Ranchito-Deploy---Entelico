How to Choose the Right Valentine’s Bouquet

If you’re deciding what to send, think beyond color alone. Consider the recipient’s style, the setting where the flowers will be displayed, and the message you want the arrangement to communicate. A dramatic dozen red roses makes a bold romantic statement, while a soft pastel arrangement may feel more intimate and nuanced.

For classic romance

Choose red roses with premium greenery, perhaps paired with eucalyptus or accent blooms. This style is ideal if you want to express passion and tradition without hesitation.

For a softer, elevated look

Choose blush, ivory, coral, or champagne tones. These combinations are especially well suited for Mission Hills homes with warm architectural details, Spanish-style interiors, and sunlight-filled living spaces.

For long-lasting elegance

Consider orchids, lilies, alstroemeria, and mixed seasonal arrangements with hardy blooms. These can hold up beautifully in homes or offices where the bouquet may be enjoyed all day or throughout the week.
