How to Choose the Right Flowers for the Season

Seasonality is one of the most important considerations when designing wedding bouquets in Granada Hills. The Valley’s climate can be forgiving in the cooler months and more demanding in the summer, so flower choice should be strategic. We guide couples toward blooms that fit the season, the color story, and the expected conditions of the day.

Spring Weddings

Spring in Granada Hills often brings ideal conditions for soft romantic flowers. Peonies, ranunculus, sweet peas, garden roses, and hydrangea can create a lush and graceful look. These flowers pair beautifully with the fresh greenery and brighter skies common in the season.

Summer Weddings

Summer weddings require flowers with strong hydration and good heat tolerance. Roses, orchids, carnations, alstroemeria, and certain lisianthus varieties can perform well when properly conditioned. We also recommend shorter bouquet handles and thoughtful scheduling for outdoor portraits to help preserve freshness.

Fall Weddings

For fall celebrations, Granada Hills couples often lean into richer colors and deeper textures. Burgundy, apricot, rust, cream, and muted green tones work beautifully against the warm neighborhood light and foothill scenery. This season is perfect for bouquets with depth and dimension.

Winter Weddings

Winter offers the chance to use elegant whites, deep reds, soft blushes, and evergreen accents. Cooler temperatures can help flowers last longer during transport and ceremony time, making this an excellent season for more delicate design choices.

Tips for Preserving Your Bouquet on the Big Day

Once your bouquet arrives, a few simple habits can help it look its best all day long.

  • Keep it in water or a cool shaded area until photos begin.
  • Avoid leaving it in direct sun for long periods, especially during warm Valley afternoons.
  • Hold the bouquet by the stems rather than the blooms to reduce stress on delicate petals.
  • If you are doing first-look photos in a neighborhood park or hillside overlook, schedule them efficiently so the bouquet spends less time exposed to heat.
  • Ask about a toss bouquet if you want to preserve your primary arrangement as a keepsake.

These small steps can make a noticeable difference in how your bouquet looks from the ceremony aisle to the final dance.
