How to Choose the Right Custom Bouquet

If you are unsure what to order, think about four things: the occasion, the recipient’s style, the preferred colors, and the display location. A bouquet intended for a bright kitchen near a sunny Toluca Lake patio may benefit from sturdier blooms and lively tones. A bouquet for a formal office near the studio district might be better in whites, blush, cream, or deep jewel shades.

It also helps to consider fragrance. Some recipients love strong floral scent, while others prefer a softer aromatic profile. The same goes for arrangement shape. Round and compact bouquets feel classic, while airy, asymmetrical designs feel more modern and artistic.

Quick selection guide

  • For romance: roses, ranunculus, tulips, and soft accent flowers
  • For celebration: bright mixed seasonal flowers with dynamic color contrast
  • For elegance: orchids, calla lilies, hydrangea, and refined greenery
  • For comfort: white blooms, pale neutrals, and serene textures
