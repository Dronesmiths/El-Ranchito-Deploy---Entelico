How to Choose the Right Wedding Flowers

Picking wedding flowers can feel overwhelming, especially when you are balancing color themes, seasonality, budget, and venue style. A smart approach is to start with the overall atmosphere you want your wedding to create, then build the floral plan around that vision.

Start with your color story

Choose colors that work well with your attire, décor, and venue surroundings. Chatsworth’s natural landscape pairs beautifully with ivory, blush, terracotta, sage, champagne, and deep green. If you are planning a sunset ceremony with mountain views, warmer tones can look especially striking in photos.

Consider flower seasonality

Seasonal blooms are often fresher, more affordable, and more resilient. We help couples select flowers that are at their best during their wedding month, which improves both appearance and performance. Seasonal planning is especially important in the valley, where heat can shorten the lifespan of some delicate varieties.

Match the scale to the venue

A smaller backyard wedding near Mason Avenue may need fewer large arrangements and more strategic floral accents, while a larger venue may benefit from dramatic entrance pieces and layered reception florals. The goal is to create balance without overcrowding the space.
