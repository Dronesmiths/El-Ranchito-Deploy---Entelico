The Custom Bouquet Design Process

We make the process simple without making it generic. Every custom bouquet begins with a conversation about the occasion, preferred colors, flower types, budget, and overall mood. From there, we build an arrangement that feels cohesive and elevated.

1. Share Your Vision

Tell us the basics: who the bouquet is for, what the occasion is, and what style you have in mind. You do not need to know flower names to get started. You can describe the feeling you want—romantic, cheerful, elegant, modern, classic, or dramatic—and we’ll translate that into a floral design.

2. Choose the Palette

Color is one of the most important parts of bouquet design. Soft blush and cream tones feel timeless. Reds and burgundies create intensity. Sunflower yellow, coral, and orange bring energy. White and green compositions feel clean and sophisticated. We can also match school colors, event themes, or personal preferences.

3. Select the Flowers

Depending on seasonality and availability, we may recommend roses, lilies, hydrangeas, tulips, carnations, ranunculus, orchids, alstroemeria, or mixed seasonal stems. We also pay attention to texture and shape so the bouquet has depth, movement, and visual balance.

4. Finish the Presentation

Presentation matters. A bouquet for an upscale dinner in the foothills may call for premium wrapping and a modern finish. A cheerful gift for a friend near Balboa Boulevard may be better suited to a softer, more playful wrap. We tailor the final look so the bouquet feels complete from every angle.
